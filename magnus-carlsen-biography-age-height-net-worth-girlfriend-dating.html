Magnus Carlsen Age & Early Life

Magnus Carlsen was born in Tnsberg, Vestfold, Norway on November 30, 1990. His parents, Sigrun en and Henrik Albert Carlsen, are also engineers. When he was a toddler, he began to demonstrate his cerebral ability. He could complete 50-piece jigsaw puzzles on his own at the age of two and proceeded to construct Lego sets designed for considerably older youngsters at the age of four. His father saw the boy’s intellect and competitive nature and taught him to play chess.

Magnus Carlsen Net Worth in 2023

How much is Magnus Carlsen’s net worth? According to Celebritynetwoth, Magnus Carlsen’s net worth was estimated to reach $8 million as of October 2023, with yearly profits of $2 million. In 2020, he won the ‘Chess24’ championship, earning $510,587 in prize money. He was the highest-paid esports player in the world as of 2021.

Career

He received worldwide prominence in 2004 after winning the Corus chess tournament in Wijk aan Zee in the C group. Carlsen was just a teenager at the time, but his triumph in the C group earned him the moniker “Mozart of Chess” from Grandmaster Lubomir Kavalek. In March 2004, he beat the former world champion, Anatoly Karpov, in a blitz chess event and subsequently drew with Garry Kasparov, the world’s top-rated player. Carlsen was booted out of the competition after being beaten by Kasparov in another round.

In April 2004, he placed second in the Dubai Open Chess Championship and was promoted to Grandmaster. He was the world’s youngest Grandmaster at the time, and the third-youngest person ever to hold the GM position. Magnus performed well in the 2006 Norwegian Chess Championship but was defeated in the final round by Berge Stansted. He did, however, win the championship for the first time in the play-off. Magnus Carlsen competed against world chess champion Viswanathan Anand in the World Chess Championship in Chennai, India, in 2013. Carlsen won the tournament with a score of 612–312 after winning games five, six, and nine and drawing the rest. As a result, he became the new world chess champion, the second-youngest player (behind Kasparov) to do so. In November 2014, he played Anand again in a match for the title of World Chess Champion and successfully defended his title.

In 2007, he faced numerous strong opponents in the famed Linares chess tournament, which many see as “the Wimbledon of chess.” Carlsen finished second in a tournament against top-rated players including Veselin Topalov, Viswanathan Anand, Peter Svidler, Alexander Morozevich, and Levon Aronian.

Djonne used to follow him to his events, and she was there when he won the 2019 championship in Moscow.
